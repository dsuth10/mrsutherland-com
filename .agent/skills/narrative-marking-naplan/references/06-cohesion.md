# Cohesion Criterion (0-4 points)

**Skill focus:** The control of multiple threads and relationships over the whole text, achieved through the use of referring words, substitutions, word associations and text connectives.

---

## Score: 0
**Category:** Symbols or drawings
- Cannot be read

---

## Score: 1
**Category:** Links are missing or incorrect
- Links are missing or incorrect
- Short script
- Often confusing for the reader

---

## Score: 2
**Category:** Some correct links between sentences
- Some correct links between sentences (do not penalise for poor punctuation)
- Most referring words are accurate
- Reader may occasionally need to re-read and provide their own links to clarify meaning

**Characteristics:**
- Small selection of simple connectives and conjunctions used: then, soon, and, but, or, then, suddenly, so, and then, when, ordinal numbers, only temporal connectives
- Often marked by cumbersome repetition of nouns or unreferenced pronouns

---

## Score: 3
**Category:** Cohesive devices used correctly
- Cohesive devices are used correctly to support reader understanding
- Accurate use of referring words
- Meaning is clear and text flows well in a sustained piece of writing

**Examples of connectives:**
- later, meanwhile, instead, in the middle of, earlier, just as, usually, although, even though, such as, because, finally
- Word association to avoid repetition: synonyms, antonyms, word sets
- Control of narrative tense

---

## Score: 4
**Category:** Range of cohesive devices used deliberately
- A range of cohesive devices is used correctly and deliberately to enhance reading
- An extended, highly cohesive piece of writing showing continuity of ideas and tightly linked sections of text
- Consistent use of word associations and substitutions that enhance reading
